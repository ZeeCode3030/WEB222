Scrabble

History

Scrabble was originally called Criss Cross. The game created by Alfred M. Butts in 1931. It was based on crossword puzzles and anagrams. It was eventually re-branded and marketed as Scrabble by James Brunot in 1948. It made its first sale in Great Britain in 1954.

Later, Scrabble continued to be one of the most popular board and tile games in the US and was developed in numerous foreign languages, Braille, and magnetic editions. Since 1973, competitions have been held in the US.

How to Play

Scrabble is a board and tile game in which two to four players compete by spelling out words on a 225-square board using lettered tiles; the words form a crossword-like pattern.

At the beginning and after each turn, players take seven tiles from a pool to start. A player can only see his own tiles on the board and the tiles in the pool that are kept secret from the other players. Any or all of a player's tiles may be exchanged for tiles in the pool in order to forfeit their turn. There are 100 letter tiles with a point value for each letter that roughly corresponds to how frequently that letter appears in English words.

Words are scored by adding the point values of their letters and multiplying them by one of the 61 premium squares available, which include double letter, triple letter, double word, and triple word. Scoring is possible both horizontally and vertically as the game progresses, with better scores recorded by forming two or more interlocking words at the same time. When one player has no tiles left or the board is deadlocked at the end of the game, the player with the most points wins. The values of unused letters left to players are added together and subtracted from their scores.

Tips and Tricks

  • Learn your words especially ones with no vowels
  • Use your S's wisely: 90% of the words you come up with will get gain you more points if you add an S afterwards
  • Don't underestimate short words: if you are in a bind and only have short words, use them. They will accumulate and boost your point
  • Rearrange your tiles occasionally
  • Learn words that have hardly occurring letters like Q, X, J
